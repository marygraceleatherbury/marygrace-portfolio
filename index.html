01 — About

A technologist with a brand mindset.

My Story

I study Computer Science at Clemson University, where I'm pursuing a minor in Brand Communications and expecting to graduate in May 2028. My path is intentionally interdisciplinary: I want to build products that are not only well-engineered but thoughtfully communicated — where the thing being made and the way it reaches people are designed together.

My interests sit at the intersection of product management and UX research. I care about understanding why people use what they use, then translating that insight into decisions a team can actually ship. Outside the technical side, I'm a long-time advocate for women in STEM and believe representation is a design problem worth solving in its own right.

When I'm not coding or writing briefs, you'll find me refining a Notion system, overthinking typography, or practicing my French.

Project 01

Brand Comms · GC 3700

Fahlo — A Brand Playbook

Brand strategy and anthem storyboard for the wildlife tracking bracelet company.

Challenge

Fahlo has a compelling purpose — every bracelet funds wildlife tracking — but needed a sharper articulation of how that purpose lives across touchpoints and campaigns.

Solution

Built a full Brand Playbook and an anthem video storyboard that ties product, purpose, and storytelling into one cohesive system teams can activate.

Insight

People don't just want to buy — they want to belong to something that matters.

Strategy

Lead with the animal's story; the product becomes the proof.

A brand system that turns each bracelet from an accessory into a chapter of a shared conservation story.

Role — Brand Strategy, Storyboarding Tools — Canva, Adobe Suite

02
CS · CPSC 2150

Ataxx — A Board Game in Java

Object-oriented implementation and testing of a two-player strategy game.

Challenge

Design a clean, testable Java implementation of the Ataxx board game that holds up against an autograder requiring exact method signatures — while collaborating across a three-person team.

Solution

Architected the board logic with clear primary/secondary method separation, then wrote the JUnit test suite covering core gameplay, including placement validation and end-game detection.

Insight

Good tests aren't an afterthought — they're the spec.

Strategy

Let the interface drive the implementation, not the other way around.

A collaboratively-built Java game that passed on structure, tests, and team coordination.

Role — Development, JUnit Testing Partners — Sydney, Riley
